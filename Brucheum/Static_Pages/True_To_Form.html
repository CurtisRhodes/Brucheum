Rhetoric

the word rhetoric has been given a bad name. Fancy evasive empty talk is dismissed as labeling it rhetoric. Most people today would define rhetoric as "empty meaningless talk". Actually, empty talk is the opposite of rhetoric, or at least not very good rhetoric. Because the traditional definition of the word rhetoric is closer to "The Art of Persuasion" Selling an insurance policy is persuasion, asking someone out on a date, taking a job interview, getting elected to office is the art of persuasion and an exercise is rhetoric. Advocating for your position online, in a business conference room or on the floor of the general assembly the art of persuasion is rhetoric.

Aristotle wrote a book about it as have many others. Debate clubs practice the art. Appeals to reason and appeals to ethics are considered but all agree logic and morals are never more effective than plays to emotion.

Recognizing the massive importance of rhetoric in so much of human activity from making a deal to getting a job to convincing your friends which restarunt to go for dinner, I'm sorry to report there may be a dark side to the ancient and respected art of rhetoric.

There's a small problem. Sophisticated practitioners have discovered effective ways to manipulate people by inflaming their emotions. Masters of the art of persuasion have a tremendous power.

Sleazy Ted is good at his own form of ridicule and fear mongering, racially coded, hate drenched word craft that qualifies as rhetoric.
